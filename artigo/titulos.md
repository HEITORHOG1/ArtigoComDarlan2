# Five candidate titles — *Automation in Construction*

Target: Self-Aware Steel Structures / Structural Cognition (digital twin + ML + SHM).

**Recommended (used for the full draft): Title 1.**

## 1. From Passive Members to Self-Aware Steel Structures: A Digital-Twin and Machine-Learning Framework for Structural Cognition and Autonomous Health Monitoring  ⭐ **(recommended)**

- **Angle:** Integrative framework / paradigm-shift umbrella — pairs the passive-to-cognitive contrast with the full digital-twin + ML + SHM + steel keyword set, positioning the article as the reference conceptual framework.
- **Journal fit:** Excellent: carries every Automation in Construction index keyword (digital twin, machine learning, structural health monitoring, autonomous, steel structures) and frames the work as automation/digitalization of built-environment assets; 'Framework' honestly signals a conceptual contribution rather than experimental results.

## 2. Structural Cognition: A New Paradigm for Self-Aware Steel Structures That Perceive, Interpret and Autonomously Report Their Own Mechanical State

- **Angle:** Cognition / paradigm-centric — foregrounds the novel 'Structural Cognition' concept and the perceive-interpret-report loop that distinguishes the proposal from conventional data-only SHM.
- **Journal fit:** Strong on novelty and memorability; uses 'autonomous' and 'steel structures' but omits the explicit 'digital twin'/'machine learning' index terms, so it sells the idea more than the indexable methodology, making it a slightly weaker scope match than the integrative title.

## 3. Cognitive Digital Twins for Autonomous Damage Detection in Steel Structures: A Conceptual Framework and State-of-the-Art Review

- **Angle:** Digital-twin + autonomous-damage-detection centric — leads with the cognitive-digital-twin construct (FEM-as-twin fed by sensors) and the damage-detection payoff, with explicit 'review' positioning.
- **Journal fit:** Very strong scope alignment: digital twin, autonomous, damage detection and steel structures are all core journal terms, and 'Conceptual Framework and State-of-the-Art Review' precisely and honestly describes the no-experiments-yet article type favored for review contributions.

## 4. Industry 4.0 and Embedded Intelligence for Steel Infrastructure: A Reference Architecture Enabling Self-Aware Structures and Predictive Maintenance

- **Angle:** Industry 4.0 / automation and reference-architecture centric — frames the proposal as a deployable digitalization architecture (sensing, DAQ, twin, AI) tied to predictive maintenance and lifecycle value.
- **Journal fit:** Good fit with the journal's automation/information-technology and operation-and-maintenance scope; 'reference architecture' and 'predictive maintenance' resonate with built-environment automation, though it leans on Industry 4.0 framing over the more heavily indexed 'machine learning'/'SHM' terms.

## 5. A Hybrid Physics-Informed and Deep-Learning Architecture for Self-Aware Steel Structures: Autoencoders and LSTM Networks Coupled with Finite-Element Digital Twins

- **Angle:** ML-architecture / methods centric — specifies the technical stack (physics-informed learning, autoencoders, LSTM, FEM-based digital twin), directly mapping to Brunton & Kutz, Naser and Cook et al.
- **Journal fit:** Methodologically precise and rich in indexable ML/digital-twin terms; well grounded in the books, but its concrete-method specificity risks promising algorithmic detail or results that a conceptual framework paper does not yet deliver, making it a better fit once experiments exist.

---

**Why title 1 was chosen:** Index 0 is the strongest fit for both the books' strengths and the journal scope because it is the only title that simultaneously names all of the article's load-bearing pillars and the journal's most-indexed keywords: digital twin, machine learning, structural health monitoring, autonomous, and steel structures. As a conceptual-framework-plus-review paper, the article's core selling point is the paradigm shift it proposes, and the 'From Passive Members to Self-Aware Steel Structures' opening captures that contrast explicitly (echoing Farrar & Worden's two-state/condition-interpretation framing and Chopra's mass-stiffness-frequency basis for detecting change), while 'A Digital-Twin and Machine-Learning Framework' signals the model-plus-data fusion that Brunton & Kutz and Naser ground rigorously and that Cook et al. supply the FEM baseline for. The word 'Framework' honestly signals this is conceptual, not experimental, avoiding any overclaim of results. It centers 'Structural Cognition' (the paper's named novelty) and 'Autonomous Health Monitoring' (the functional payoff Automation in Construction cares about), giving reviewers and indexers a precise, non-clickbait, scope-aligned title. The competing titles each emphasize a single facet (cognition-only, damage-detection-only, Industry 4.0-only, or ML-architecture-only) and therefore index less completely; title 0 is the best umbrella for a framework paper whose contribution is the integration itself.
